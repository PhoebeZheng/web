  • 1. This celebration of love dates back to Ancient Rome
  • 2. Heart shaped boxes of chocolate was first mass produced in 1861 by Richard Cadbury, the fancy packaging increased sales. Today around 36 million boxes are sold a year.
  • 3. Conversation hearts started out as medical lozenges. In 1847, Oliver Chase created a machine that simplified the process of making lozenges, and turned his focus onto creating candy. He founded Necco, and started creating candy called Necco wafers. In 1866, his brother Daniel started printing messages onto them. By 1902, they made them heart-shaped
  • 4. Consumers spend an estimated $19.6 billion on Valentine goods yearly, $1.8b on candy alone, and $4.7b on jewelry!
  • 5. Since 2010, spending on Valentine's day gifts for friends has tripled, Galentine's day, a day before Valentine's, was created by a fictional character: Leslie Knope of Parks and Recreation. It’s a day dedicated to showering your closest friends with love and attention...whether they are single or not.
  • 6. In the late 17th century, giving flowers became a popular custom. The practice can be traced back to when King Charles II of Sweden learned the "language of flowers" on a trip to Persia, and then introduced the tradition to Europe. The act of giving flowers subsequently became a popular trend during the Victorian Era — including on Valentine's Day — with red roses symbolizing deep love.
